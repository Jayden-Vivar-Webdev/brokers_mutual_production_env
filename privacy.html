Privacy Policy

Brokers Mutual Acceptance Pty Ltd (BMA)

Our commitment to protect your privacy: We understand how important it is to protect your personal information. This policy sets out our privacy policy commitment in respect of personal information we hold about you and what we do with that information.

We recognise that any personal information we collect about you will only be used for the purposes we have collected it or as allowed under the law. It is important to us that you are confident that any personal information we hold about you will be treated in a way which ensures protection of your personal information.

Our commitment in respect of personal information is to abide by the Australian Privacy Principles for the protection of personal information, as set out in the Privacy Act and the Notifiable Data Breach scheme established under the Act.

Periodically we will update our privacy policy. You can always find the most up-to-date version on our websites. By using our services, you consent to the terms of this Policy and agree to be bound by it.

What Information Do We Collect?

We collect most personal information directly from you, whether in person, on the phone, electronically, or if you deal with us in some other way.

  • Credit-related Information: Information used to assess your eligibility for finance, including finance outstanding, repayment history, and defaults.
  • Identification Information: Information such as your name, date of birth, address, account details, occupation, and other identifiers.
  • Financial Information: Information required for credit-related decisions, including credit reports, bank account details, employment status, income, expenses, assets, liabilities, and dependents.
  • Sensitive Information: Information about your religion, ethnicity, health, or criminal record, collected with your consent when necessary.

Why We Collect Your Information

We collect your personal information for the purposes of assessing your application for finance and managing that finance, which includes:

  • Verifying your identity.
  • Assisting you in obtaining approval or provision of a product or service.
  • Conducting credit-worthiness checks and fraud prevention.
  • Managing risks and identifying illegal activity.
  • Providing updates about finance and new offers.

Direct Marketing

From time to time, we may use your personal information to provide you with information about finance, offers, and new products. You can opt-out by contacting us at any time via phone or email.

How We Collect Your Personal Information

We collect personal information from various sources, including directly from you, joint applicants, and third parties such as CRBs and referral partners.

How We Disclose Your Personal Information

We may disclose your personal information to third parties such as banks, lenders, service providers, and law enforcement agencies as required by law.

Transfer of Information Overseas

Your personal information may be disclosed to entities outside Australia, including service providers located in New Zealand, the Philippines, and the USA.

Updating Your Personal Information

It is important that we maintain accurate information. You may contact us to update or correct your details at any time.

How We Protect Your Information

We take reasonable steps to secure your personal information from misuse, interference, and loss, using methods such as firewalls, encryption, and access control.

Complaints & Further Information

If you have a complaint regarding your personal information, please contact our complaints officer at complaints@brokersmutual.com.au.

You can also make a complaint to the Privacy Commissioner at www.oaic.gov.au.
